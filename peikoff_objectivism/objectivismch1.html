Glossary

We begin by defining “true” (Definition .0.1) and “self-evidence” (Definition .0.2).

Definition .0.1 (True). In accordance with reality.

Definition .0.2 (Self-Evidence). A proposition that is known to be true by sense perception, without the need of proof.

Now, we can define “axiom”, “corollary”, “theorem” and “proof” (Definitions .0.3, .0.4, .0.5 and .0.6).

Definition .0.3 (Axiom). A self-evidence.

Definition .0.4 (Corollary). A self-evidence that follows from another self-evidence.

Definition .0.5 (Theorem). A proposition that is not self-evident but it proved by a chain of reasoning; a truth established by means of accepted truths.

Definition .0.6 (Proof). Argument establishing the truth of a statement.

This book is about philosophy. Below are the definitions of the five branches of philosohy the will be discussed: metaphysics, epistemology, ethics, politics and aesthetics (Definitions .0.7, .0.8, .0.9, .0.10 and .0.11) - which are discussed in Parts I, II, III, IV and V respectively.

Definition .0.7 (Metaphysics). The branch of philosophy that deals with the first principles of things.

Definition .0.8 (Epistemoly). The branch of phislosophy that deals with knowledge, especially with regard to its methods, validity, and scope.

Remark. Knowledge is a thing, therefore metaphysics deals with first principles of knowledge.

Definition .0.9 (Ethics). The branch of knowledge that deals with moral principles.

Definition .0.10 (Politics). The branck of knowledge that deals with government and the state.

Definition .0.11 (Aesthetics). The branch of kanowledge that deals with the principles of beauty and art.

New concepts will be defined throughout the text.
